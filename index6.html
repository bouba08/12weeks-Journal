Week 6

Escape room project

This week, we delved into the realm of game development using Node.js, crafting our very own creation. The initial step involved meticulously planning out the game's flow by sketching a comprehensive flowchart. This blueprint ensured that our development process remained aligned with our envisioned design. To implement the escape room game, we relied on a trio of essential modules: inquirer, chalk, and emoji. Leveraging the inquirer module facilitated seamless interaction with users through prompts, enabling them to navigate options such as selecting the difficulty level. The game offered three difficulty settings: easy, medium, and hard, each presenting its unique challenges. In the easy mode, players faced a choice between three doors, one of which concealed a prize while the others harbored ghosts. Selecting the incorrect door resulted in the loss of one of three lives, a cycle repeated over five rounds. The medium and hard modes ramped up the challenge with five and seven doors, respectively. Losing all three lives spelled defeat for the player. To visually enhance the experience, we incorporated emojis, including hearts to represent lives, door icons, and ghost figures. The chalk module was instrumental in adding vibrant colors to the text, enriching the game's visual appeal. Furthermore, we structured a character class endowed with properties such as name, life, wrong, and round, complemented by functions like rightGuess, wrongGuess, wonGame, and lostGame. These elements dynamically responded to the game's progress, ensuring an immersive and engaging experience for players.

Project management

Project management software is designed to help businesses and individuals track projects, tasks and schedules. The core value of this kind of software is its ability to bring structure to complexity. It's a great tool for staying organized and ensuring your team remains on task and aligned with project objectives. While each development company may use a slightly different software process, each have at least 6 stages in common: planning, designing, development, testing, deployment, and maintenance.
